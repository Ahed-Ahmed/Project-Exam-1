Making Life Multiplanetary

Development summary

  • 2005: SpaceX begins testing Falcon 1

    Its first real rocket design created with the goal of reusable space flight

  • 2006: The first award from NASA

    SpaceX wins its first NASA award for US$278 million to help develop Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon space capsule

  • 2007: Build a new rocket factory

    SpaceX moves to Hawthorne, California, to build a new rocket factory that could handle its larger Falcon 9 rockets

  • 2008: First rocket reach the orbit

    Falcon 1 becomes the first privately funded, liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit

  • 2010: First company to successfully recover a spacecraft

    SpaceX becomes the first commercial company to successfully recover a spacecraft from Earth orbit with its Dragon spacecraft

  • 2011: Falcon Heavy

    SpaceX begins the development of Falcon Heavy

  • 2014: Crew Dragon

    SpaceX reveals its Crew Dragon

  • 2015: First probe beyond Earth orbit

    SpaceX becomes the first private company to send a probe beyond Earth orbit as well as the first to achieve landing of a first stage orbital capable rocket

  • 2017: The recovery and reuse of rockets

    SpaceX achieves one of its great ambitions, the recovery and reuse of rockets, conducting the historic first reflight of an orbital class rocket

  • 2018: Launching Falcon Heavy

    SpaceX begins launching Falcon Heavy, the world’s most powerful operational rocket by a factor of two

  • 2019: Next-generation spacecraft

    SpaceX’s next-generation spacecraft, a Starship orbital prototype, begins initial tests with success

  • 2020: The first crewed mission

    SpaceX successfully achieves its first-ever crewed mission
